About

I am a PhD student in Physics at the Harish-Chandra Research Institute (HRI), India, working in the MATES Lab on emerging oxide double perovskites under pressure. My research focuses on understanding and designing functional materials for optoelectronic and energy applications using first-principles simulations.

Before joining HRI in 2021, I was a PhD student in the Discipline of Physics at IIT Indore. I completed my M.Sc. in Physics from the University of Mysore with distinction, and my B.Sc. from JSS College for Women, also under the University of Mysore, with distinction.

Research Interests

  • Density Functional Theory (DFT) for solids and heterostructures
  • Oxide double perovskites under pressure and structural phase behavior
  • Electronic structure: band structures, DOS, effective masses, band alignment
  • Phonons, lattice dynamics, octahedral distortions, and finite-temperature properties
  • Optical properties, SLME, and materials for photovoltaics and photodetection
  • Halide perovskites, hybrid organic–inorganic perovskites, and MXene–perovskite heterostructures
  • AIMD simulations and excited charge dynamics in functional materials
